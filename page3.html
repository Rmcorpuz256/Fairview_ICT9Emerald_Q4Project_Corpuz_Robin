The Wright Brothers


The Wright brothers, Orville and Wilbur Wright, were known for their invention, also known as the first flying airplane. As we know nowadays, to go to different countries, we ride planes such as those manufactured by Boeing and Airbus, and thanks to the Wright brothers, modern planes such as the Boeing 777-9x and Airbus 350-1000 are possible. The two aviators, Orville and Wilbur Wright, were born in Dayton, Ohio, United States. As kids in Dayton, Ohio, they got really interested in flying. It wasn't just a hobby; it became their big passion that pushed them to do amazing things. This fascination wasn't only a childlike interest that would go away when they grew up, and this interest is what helped them in creating the first-ever plane to fly. The Wright brothers wanted to make a flying machine after being inspired by others like Otto Lilienthal. Lilienthal's bold glider experiments really got them excited and made them want to see how far they could go with flying.

The two aviators, Orville and Wilbur Wright, started to try to actually fly the plane. The Wright brothers worked really hard to make their dream of flying come true. They spent a ton of time doing research, trying out different ideas, and learning from their mistakes. They weren't just building a machine; they were trying to figure out how to make things fly.Their journey to flying didn't come without major challenges and setbacks. They spent many hours researching, experiencing, and experimenting, with trial and error non-stop. They never gave up their dream because they were determined to fulfill their childhood plans. They kept pushing through the rough times, and this shows how nothing is impossible if you give up easily; you won't accomplish anything, and if you do hard work like the Wright brothers, you could do anything you dream of.

Finally, after many hours of trial and error, the day of accomplishment had finally come. Near Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, after endless attempts of trying, it finally happened. During this moment, the world's aviation sector forever changed because the plane they had been working on got airborne and started flying. This accomplishment opened a new era of technology, which led to the planes that we ride to other countries today.Their accomplishment shows how hard they worked non-stop for this dream, even though there were many setbacks and errors in their plans, they still kept persisting through all the challenges. Their accomplishment was revolutionary because it made a faster way of getting to other countries instead of boats.


The Wright Flyer


The first ever aircraft created by the Wright brothers was an extraordinary creation, which changed everything for the aviation sector. During the plane's early flights in 1903, It wasn't flying that fast. It only reached a top speed of 10.9 miles per hour or rather 17.5 kilometers per hour, which isn't that fast compared to our modern-day aircraft today, but back then speed was not a big deal. The wright brothers proved that a plane could fly by using an engine that a pilot controlled.

The plane had a wingspan of approximately 12.3 meters (40 feet), and roughly 6.4 meters long (21 feet). The aircraft was powered by a four-cylinder internal combustion engine, which generated about 12 horse power. Each propeller was measured to be 2.7 meters in diameter and they rotated in opposite directions. The aircraft's weight was 340 kilograms without a pilot and fuel, but with a pilot and fuel, it increased to 605 kilograms.
